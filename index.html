Why Donate Blood?

Understanding the importance of blood donation

Save Lives

One donation can save up to three lives. Blood is needed for surgeries, cancer treatment, and emergencies.

Health Benefits

Regular blood donation helps reduce harmful iron stores and stimulates production of new blood cells.

Quick Process

The entire process takes only about 30 minutes. The actual donation takes just 8-10 minutes.

Health Tips for Donors

Essential tips before and after blood donation for a safe experience

Stay Hydrated

Drink at least 16 oz (500ml) of water before donating. Proper hydration makes the process easier.

Eat Iron-Rich Foods

Include spinach, beans, red meat, and fortified cereals in your diet 2-3 days before donation.

Get Good Sleep

Ensure 7-8 hours of quality sleep the night before. Rest helps your body prepare.

Eat a Healthy Meal

Have a nutritious meal 2-3 hours before. Avoid fatty foods as they affect blood tests.

Avoid Alcohol

No alcohol for at least 24 hours before donating. It causes dehydration.

Wear Comfortable Clothes

Wear short sleeves or sleeves that can easily roll up above your elbow.

Rest for 15 Minutes

Stay at the donation center and rest. Enjoy the refreshments provided.

Drink Extra Fluids

Drink 4-5 extra glasses of non-alcoholic beverages over the next 24 hours.

Keep Bandage On

Leave the bandage on for at least 4-5 hours. Keep the area clean and dry.

Avoid Heavy Exercise

Skip strenuous activities and heavy lifting for 24 hours after donation.

Have a Snack

Eat iron-rich snacks to help replenish your iron stores quickly.

If Feeling Dizzy

Lie down with feet elevated. Drink fluids and rest until you feel better.

Age Requirement

You must be between 18-65 years old to donate blood in India.

Weight Requirement

Minimum weight of 50 kg (110 lbs) is required for safe donation.

Hemoglobin Level

Minimum 12.5 g/dL hemoglobin is required. This is tested before donation.

Donation Gap

Men: 3 months (90 days) gap. Women: 4 months (120 days) gap between donations.

Good Health

You should be feeling well on the day. No cold, flu, or infection.

Recent Tattoo/Piercing

Wait 6-12 months after getting a tattoo or piercing before donating.

Frequently Asked Questions

Click on any question to reveal the answer

Who can donate blood?

Anyone between 18-65 years old, weighing at least 50 kg, and in good health can donate blood. You should not have any chronic diseases like diabetes, heart conditions, or blood-borne infections. A gap of at least 3 months is required between donations.

How often can I donate blood?

Men can donate blood every 3 months (12 weeks), while women can donate every 4 months (16 weeks). This ensures your body has enough time to replenish the donated blood cells.

Is blood donation safe?

Yes, blood donation is completely safe. We use sterile, single-use needles and equipment for each donor. The process is supervised by trained medical professionals. Your body replaces the donated blood within a few weeks.

What should I do before donating blood?

Before donating, make sure to: Get a good night's sleep, eat a healthy meal (avoid fatty foods), drink plenty of water, bring a valid ID, and wear comfortable clothing with sleeves that can be raised above the elbow.

What happens after I donate blood?

After donation, you'll rest for 10-15 minutes and be offered refreshments. Your blood will be tested, processed, and separated into components. Within 24-48 hours, your donation could be helping patients in need!
